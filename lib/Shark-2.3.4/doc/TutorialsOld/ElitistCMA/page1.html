The (1+1)-Evolution Strategy

The covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) is one of the most powerful evolutionary algorithms for real-valued optimization. The CMA-ES is a variable metric algorithm, which efficiently adapts its search distribution to the problem at hand. This, together with the fact that it can be reliably used with small population sizes, is the reason for the high efficiency of the CMA-ES.

The CMA-ES possesses several important invariance properties. It is solely based on the ranking of candidate solutions and therefore invariant under order-preserving transformations of the objective function. The CMA-ES is invariant under angle-preserving transformations of the input space, like translation, rotation and reflection, given that the initial solution is transformed accordingly. The algorithm is not affected by an overall rescaling of the search space, given that the initial scaling parameter is chosen accordingly. In the end, the CMA-ES is even invariant under any full rank linear transformation of the search space, given that the initial covariance matrix is also transformed accordingly.

Nikolaus Hansen and Andreas Ostermeier. Completely derandomized selfadaptation in evolution strategies. Evolutionary Computation 9(2), pp. 159-195, 2001

Several variants of the CMA-ES are implemented in the EALib. In this tutorial, we consider the elitist (1+1)-CMA-ES as an example. Elitism means that the best solution is never discarded from the population. It is generally faster for fine-tuning on unimodal functions, but for noisy and/or multimodal optimization problems the standard variant is preferable. For step size adaption, the (1+1)-CMA-ES uses an improved 1/5-th success rule, which replaces the cumulative path length control in the standard CMA-ES.

Christian Igel, Nikolaus Hansen, and Stefan Roth. Covariance Matrix Adaptation for Multi-objective Optimization. Evolutionary Computation 15(1), pp. 1-28, 2007
